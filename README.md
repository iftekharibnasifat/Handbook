# 1. BOARD LEVEL ROLES

---

## 1.1 Founder

**Role Description:**  
The Founder is the original creator of the company. They established the business idea, built the initial team, and set the company's culture and mission from the ground up.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Define the founding vision, mission, and long-term goals
- Make final decisions on company culture and core values
- Represent the company to investors, media, and the public
- Guide strategic pivots and major business decisions
- Mentor the executive team and maintain founding principles
- Oversee equity structure and shareholder relationships

**Required Skills:**
- Visionary leadership and innovation mindset
- Risk tolerance and entrepreneurial courage
- Fundraising and investor relations
- Strong communication and storytelling
- Business model design and product intuition

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Leadership → vision casting, team inspiration, culture setting
- Communication → public speaking, media relations, pitch delivery
- Business → market analysis, competitive strategy, pivot decisions
- Fundraising → investor deck preparation, term sheet negotiation

**Tools Used:**
- Notion / Confluence (documentation)
- Pitch.com / PowerPoint (investor presentations)
- Slack / Email (communication)
- Cap table tools (Carta, Pulley)

**Performance Focus:**
- Company growth and survival in early stages
- Successful fundraising rounds
- Building a strong founding team
- Product-market fit achievement

---

## 1.2 Co-Founder

**Role Description:**  
The Co-Founder shares founding ownership and responsibilities with the Founder. They typically own a specific domain (technology, marketing, operations) and are equally responsible for the company's early success.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Co-own strategic decisions alongside the Founder
- Lead a specific functional domain (CTO, CMO, etc.) as Co-Founder
- Represent the company in investor and partner meetings
- Help define company culture and values
- Recruit and retain early-stage talent
- Maintain co-founder alignment on vision and direction

**Required Skills:**
- Domain expertise (tech, business, design, etc.)
- Partnership and collaboration
- Strategic thinking
- Resilience and adaptability
- Leadership under uncertainty

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Domain expertise → deep functional knowledge, technical or commercial skill
- Collaboration → co-founder communication, conflict resolution, shared decision-making
- Leadership → early team building, culture setting, lead by example

**Tools Used:**
- Same as Founder role
- Domain-specific tools based on functional area

**Performance Focus:**
- Co-ownership of company milestones
- Domain area success (product, tech, sales, etc.)
- Maintaining strong co-founder relationship

---

## 1.3 Chairman of the Board

**Role Description:**  
The Chairman leads the Board of Directors and ensures the board functions effectively. They act as a bridge between the board and the CEO, providing governance, oversight, and strategic counsel.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Lead and facilitate board meetings
- Oversee CEO performance evaluation and compensation
- Ensure strong corporate governance practices
- Guide the board on major strategic decisions
- Represent shareholder interests
- Manage board composition and director appointments

**Required Skills:**
- Corporate governance expertise
- Strategic leadership
- Financial and legal literacy
- Stakeholder management
- Diplomacy and mediation

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Governance → board structuring, compliance oversight, fiduciary duty
- Leadership → board management, agenda setting, consensus building
- Communication → shareholder communication, CEO advisory, public statements

**Tools Used:**
- Board management platforms (Boardvantage, Diligent)
- Video conferencing tools
- Legal and compliance software

**Performance Focus:**
- Board effectiveness and governance quality
- CEO accountability and performance
- Shareholder value protection

---

## 1.4 Managing Director (MD)

**Role Description:**  
The Managing Director is responsible for the overall day-to-day management of the company or a specific division. In many companies, the MD is equivalent to the CEO but reports to the Chairman or Board.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Oversee all company operations and departments
- Set and execute business strategy in line with board direction
- Manage relationships with major clients and partners
- Drive financial performance and profitability
- Represent the company in high-level negotiations
- Ensure operational excellence across all functions

**Required Skills:**
- Executive leadership and management
- Business strategy and execution
- Financial acumen
- Relationship management
- Decision-making under pressure

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Management → cross-departmental oversight, KPI management, team accountability
- Strategy → market positioning, competitive analysis, growth planning
- Finance → P&L management, budget control, investment decisions

**Tools Used:**
- ERP systems (SAP, Oracle)
- CRM (Salesforce)
- Financial dashboards (Tableau, Power BI)
- Communication tools

**Performance Focus:**
- Revenue growth and profitability
- Operational efficiency
- Strategic goal achievement

---

## 1.5 Board Director (Independent)

**Role Description:**  
An Independent Board Director is an external member of the board who provides unbiased guidance, oversight, and expertise. They have no operational role in the company and represent an objective perspective.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Attend and participate in board meetings
- Review and approve major financial and strategic decisions
- Serve on board committees (audit, compensation, governance)
- Provide independent counsel to the CEO and board
- Protect minority shareholder interests
- Evaluate CEO and senior leadership performance

**Required Skills:**
- Industry expertise or functional expertise
- Independent judgment and integrity
- Financial and legal literacy
- Governance knowledge
- Strong communication

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Independence → unbiased evaluation, conflict-of-interest management
- Expertise → industry knowledge, functional specialization
- Governance → committee leadership, compliance review

**Tools Used:**
- Board management software
- Financial analysis tools
- Document review platforms

**Performance Focus:**
- Quality of board decisions
- Governance compliance
- Effective oversight of management

---

## 1.6 Executive Director

**Role Description:**  
The Executive Director bridges the board and executive management. They implement the board's decisions and ensure strategies are executed at the operational level.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Implement board-approved strategies
- Manage senior executive team
- Report operational performance to the board
- Develop organizational policies
- Ensure regulatory compliance
- Lead organizational change initiatives

**Required Skills:**
- Strategic implementation
- Senior leadership
- Policy development
- Change management
- Communication

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Implementation → project execution, resource allocation, milestone tracking
- Policy → SOP creation, compliance enforcement, review cycles

**Tools Used:**
- Project management tools (Asana, Jira)
- ERP systems
- Reporting dashboards

**Performance Focus:**
- Strategy execution rate
- Organizational efficiency
- Policy compliance rate

---

## 1.7 Non-Executive Director

**Role Description:**  
A Non-Executive Director (NED) participates in board governance without involvement in day-to-day operations. They offer strategic advice, oversight, and industry perspective.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Participate in board oversight and governance
- Challenge and support executive team decisions
- Serve on specialized board committees
- Provide external perspective on strategic matters
- Monitor company performance against targets
- Ensure accountability of management

**Required Skills:**
- Broad business experience
- Critical thinking and challenge mindset
- Sector knowledge
- Ethical leadership

**Performance Focus:**
- Board accountability
- Governance quality
- Strategic advisory impact

---

## 1.8 Angel Investor / Seed Investor (Board Observer)

**Role Description:**  
Early-stage investors who provided funding and may hold board observer rights. They offer mentorship, networks, and financial oversight without formal voting power.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Monitor company performance and financials
- Provide strategic introductions and networks
- Advise on fundraising and investor relations
- Attend board meetings as observer
- Support founder and executive team

**Performance Focus:**
- Return on investment (ROI)
- Portfolio company growth

# 2. C-SUITE / EXECUTIVE LEVEL

---

## 2.1 Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

**Role Description:**  
The CEO is the highest-ranking executive in the company, responsible for the overall strategy, performance, and direction of the organization. They are accountable to the Board of Directors and represent the company to all stakeholders.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Set and communicate the company's vision and strategic direction
- Lead and manage the entire C-suite team
- Drive company-wide performance and growth
- Build and maintain relationships with investors, partners, and key clients
- Make final decisions on major business matters
- Represent the company publicly and in media

**Required Skills:**
- Visionary leadership
- Strategic thinking and execution
- Financial literacy and business acumen
- Stakeholder communication
- Crisis management
- Team building and culture development

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Leadership → decision-making, team inspiration, conflict resolution, culture building
- Strategy → market analysis, competitive intelligence, growth planning, pivot decisions
- Communication → board presentations, public speaking, media relations, investor updates
- Finance → P&L understanding, budget approval, investment evaluation

**Tools Used:**
- ERP/CRM (Salesforce, SAP)
- Financial reporting dashboards (Tableau, Power BI)
- Communication (Slack, Zoom, Email)
- Strategy tools (OKR platforms — Lattice, Betterworks)

**Performance Focus:**
- Revenue growth year-over-year
- Achievement of strategic OKRs
- Employee satisfaction and retention
- Investor confidence and valuation

---

## 2.2 Chief Operating Officer (COO)

**Role Description:**  
The COO is the second-in-command and is responsible for daily operations of the company. They translate the CEO's vision into operational execution and ensure the company runs efficiently.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Oversee all day-to-day business operations
- Design and improve operational processes and workflows
- Manage department heads and cross-functional collaboration
- Monitor operational KPIs and performance metrics
- Implement company-wide policies and procedures
- Support the CEO in strategic planning and execution

**Required Skills:**
- Operational management and process optimization
- Leadership and cross-functional collaboration
- Data-driven decision-making
- Project and change management
- Problem-solving under pressure

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Operations → process design, workflow optimization, resource allocation
- Leadership → department coordination, accountability systems, performance management
- Analytics → KPI tracking, operational reporting, efficiency measurement
- Change Management → policy rollout, restructuring, team alignment

**Tools Used:**
- ERP systems (SAP, Oracle, NetSuite)
- Project management (Asana, Monday.com, Jira)
- Business intelligence (Tableau, Power BI)
- Communication (Slack, Microsoft Teams)

**Performance Focus:**
- Operational efficiency and cost reduction
- On-time delivery of company goals
- Process improvement outcomes
- Cross-departmental alignment

---

## 2.3 Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

**Role Description:**  
The CFO manages the financial health of the company, including planning, risk, reporting, and compliance. They advise the CEO and board on all financial matters.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Oversee financial planning, budgeting, and forecasting
- Manage financial reporting and compliance
- Lead investor relations and fundraising activities
- Assess and manage financial risk
- Oversee accounting, tax, and treasury functions
- Guide investment and capital allocation decisions

**Required Skills:**
- Financial analysis and modeling
- Accounting and regulatory compliance
- Risk management
- Strategic financial planning
- Investor communication

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Finance → budgeting, cash flow management, P&L analysis, forecasting
- Compliance → GAAP/IFRS standards, tax law, audit management
- Risk → financial risk assessment, hedging strategies, insurance
- Investor Relations → pitch decks, financial disclosures, board reporting

**Tools Used:**
- Accounting software (QuickBooks, Xero, SAP)
- Financial planning (Anaplan, Adaptive Insights)
- Reporting (Tableau, Excel, Power BI)
- ERP systems

**Performance Focus:**
- Financial health indicators (cash flow, burn rate, runway)
- Budget vs. actual performance
- Audit outcomes
- Fundraising success

---

## 2.4 Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

**Role Description:**  
The CTO leads the company's technology strategy, infrastructure, and engineering teams. They ensure technology choices align with business goals and keep the company competitive.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Define the technology vision and roadmap
- Oversee software development and engineering teams
- Manage infrastructure, security, and DevOps
- Evaluate and adopt new technologies
- Collaborate with product teams on technical feasibility
- Represent technology in board and executive discussions

**Required Skills:**
- Deep technical expertise (software, systems, architecture)
- Technology strategy and roadmap planning
- Engineering team leadership
- Cybersecurity awareness
- Communication of technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Technical → system architecture, software engineering, cloud infrastructure, security
- Leadership → engineering team management, hiring technical talent, code culture
- Strategy → tech stack decisions, build vs. buy analysis, R&D direction
- Communication → technical documentation, board briefings, vendor negotiations

**Tools Used:**
- GitHub / GitLab (code management)
- AWS / GCP / Azure (cloud)
- Jira / Linear (engineering management)
- Architecture tools (Lucidchart, draw.io)
- Monitoring (Datadog, New Relic)

**Performance Focus:**
- System uptime and reliability
- Development velocity and release quality
- Security incident rate
- Technology cost efficiency

**Role Description:**  
The CMO is responsible for the company's marketing strategy, brand identity, and customer acquisition. They drive awareness, engagement, and growth through marketing initiatives.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Develop and execute the overall marketing strategy
- Oversee brand identity, messaging, and positioning
- Lead digital marketing, content, PR, and campaign efforts
- Manage marketing budget and ROI measurement

- Market research

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Brand → brand voice, visual identity, messaging consistency
- Digital → SEO/SEM, social media, email marketing, paid ads
- Analytics → marketing attribution, funnel analysis, CAC/LTV tracking
- Leadership → creative team management, agency oversight, campaign planning

**Tools Used:**
- HubSpot / Marketo (marketing automation)
- Google Analytics / GA4
- SEMrush / Ahrefs (SEO)
- Meta Ads / Google Ads Manager

## 2.6 Chief Human Resources Officer (CHRO)

**Role Description:**  
The CHRO leads the people strategy of the company, including talent acquisition, employee development, culture, and organizational design.

**Key Responsibilities:**
- Develop and execute the HR strategy aligned with business goals
- Oversee recruitment, onboarding, and talent management
- Build and maintain company culture and employee engagement
- Manage compensation, benefits, and performance systems
- Ensure compliance with labor laws and HR regulations
- Drive leadership development and succession planning

**Required Skills:**
- Talent management and strategic HR
- Employment law and compliance
- Organizational development
- Employee relations and engagement
- Data-driven HR analytics

**Sub-Skills Breakdown:**
- Talent → sourcing strategy, interview design, onboarding, retention programs
- Culture → values alignment, DEI initiatives, engagement surveys
- Compliance → labor law, policy creation, grievance handling
- Analytics → HR metrics, attrition analysis, headcount planning

**Tools Used:**
- HRIS (Workday, BambooHR, HiBob)
- Recruitment (LinkedIn Recruiter, Greenhouse, Lever)
- Performance management (Lattice, 15Five)
- Payroll (ADP, Gusto)

**Performance Focus:**
- Employee retention and turnover rate
- Time-to-hire and quality of hire
- Employee satisfaction (eNPS)
- HR compliance record
